(#) House Rules (##) Mechanical Rules - __General__ - I will not keep track of strict XP gains from certain events. I will level up the party after you have finished certain chapters of a story designated by myself. This makes it easier for me to create level appropriate content for the party and also lessens the hassle of XP accounting. - Initiative is rolled with 1d12 instead of 1d20. - If a die lands on the floor, reroll it. - __Random Encounters__ - Travelling - When travelling through terrain in long distances, the party must roll a certain number of dice per unit traveled in order to determine if there is a random encounter. The level of the party also decreases the chance for having a random encounter. - The party travels from place to place in the overworld through a route of hexagonal tiles. Each hexagonal tile represents approximately 1 mile of travel. The party is able to travel about 3 miles per hour of walking or about 26 miles per day. - Each tile has a challenge rating based on how dangerous the area within that tile is. This number determines how many 1d12 dice the party must roll when travelling through that specific tile. If the party rolls 1d12 and the result is an 11 or 12, then the party will receive a random encounter appropriate for that tile. - The total level of the party allows the party to subtract a certain amount from the CR of each individual tile passed through. At the 1st level, this amount is 0, and it increases by 1 at the 4th, 8th, 12th, 16th, and 20th level thereafter, culminating in a maximum of 5. For example, if the total party level if 5, the resulting reduction is 1. If this party travelled through a tile with CR 2, the CR would be reduced to 1, and only 1d12 would have to be rolled. If the total party level was 3, the CR for the tile would be 2, and the party would have to roll 2d12. - Resting - When resting in a dangerous area, such as a dungeon or the wilderness, the party must roll to determine if there is a random encounter. The party rolls a specific number of 1d12 dice, with an 11 or 12 resulting in a random encounter appropriate for the area. - For a short rest in a moderately dangerous area, such as the wilderness, the party does not have to roll for a random encounter. For a long rest in such an area, the party must roll 1d12. - For a short rest in a dungeon, battlefield, or other type of more dangerous area, the party must roll 1d12. For a long rest in such an area, the party must roll 2d12. - (Optional Rule: When rolling for any random encounter, when a 10 is the result, the party notices a potential threat before it is upon them, allowing them to interact with the encounter or dismiss it if they wish.) - __Character Creation__ - When rolling stats, roll 4d6 six times. Each time, add up the highest three dice, rerolling 1s once. You may reroll the lowest stat of the group of six once. Then you may assign your stats. - You may select any bonus with any familiar. - __Skills__ - I will be using [Background Skills](https://www.d20pfsrd.com/skills/background-skills/) rules, so make sure you read up on these if you would like to take advantage of them. - __Roleplaying__ - Certain special actions (mostly just ones that the DM likes and are subjective to the DM as good) will result in the player receiving an Advantage Token. An Advantage Token allows the player to roll advantage on any one roll decided before rolling. A player may only have one Advantage Token at any time, and it cannot be spent on other players’ rolls. - Performing a meaningful roleplay moment. - Doing something that is extremely in character even if it’s not the best course of action in terms of maximizing benefit to the player. - Describing a spell or action the player is performing with great detail. - Saying a quippy one-liner before defeating an opponent. - Staying in character and RPing with other players. - __Feats and Abilities__ - When firing a ranged attack into two or more opponents engaged in melee where one is an ally, instead of taking a -4 to attack rolls, you may take a move action to aim and then a standard action to fire at no penalty. The feat Precise Shot is removed. The new feat __Seasoned Marksman__ makes the action to aim a free action, essentially removing the penalty one would take. - Power Attack and Deadly Aim are being removed and replaced with a new baseline mechanic available to all players with +1 BAB, __Risky Strike__. - You can choose to take a –1 penalty on all attack rolls and combat maneuver checks to gain a +2 bonus on all damage rolls. This bonus to damage is increased by half (+50%) if you are making an attack with a two-handed weapon, a one handed weapon using two hands, or a primary natural weapon that adds 1-1/2 times your Strength modifier on damage rolls. This bonus to damage is halved (–50%) if you are making an attack with an offhand weapon or secondary natural weapon. When your base attack bonus reaches +4, and every 4 points thereafter, the penalty increases by –1 and the bonus to damage increases by +2. You must choose to use Risky Strike before making an attack roll, and its effects last until your next turn. The bonus damage does not apply to touch attacks or effects that do not deal hit point damage. - Combat Expertise is being removed and replaced with a new baseline mechanic available to all players with +1 BAB, __Defensive Stance__. - You can choose to take a -1 penalty on melee attack rolls and combat maneuver checks to gain a +1 dodge bonus to your Armor Class. When your base attack bonus reaches +4, the penalty increases by -1 and the dodge bonus increases by +1. You must choose to use Defensive Stance before making an attack roll and its effects last until your next turn. - Improved Two-Weapon Fighting is now removed and is merged with an altered version of __Greater Two-Weapon Fighting__. - Prerequisites are now Dex 17, Two-Weapon Fighting, and BAB +6. In addition to the standard single extra attack you get with an off-hand weapon, you get a second attack with it, albeit at a –5 penalty. Once your BAB reaches +11, you also gain a third attack with your off-hand weapon, albeit at a –10 penalty. - Mobility and Dodge are now merged into a new version of __Mobile Dodge__. - You gain a +1 dodge bonus to your AC. This bonus increases to +4 against attacks of opportunity caused when you move out of or within a threatened tile. A condition that makes you lose your Dex bonus to AC also makes you lose the benefits of this feat. - Improved Bull Rush, Improved Drag, Improved Overrun, Improved Sunder are now replaced with __Powerful Maneuvers__. - You do not provoke an attack of opportunity when performing a bull rush, drag, overrun, or sunder combat maneuver. In addition, you receive a +2 bonus on checks with these combat maneuvers. Now a prerequisite for the relevant greater combat maneuver feats. - Improved Trip, Improved Disarm, Improved Dirty Trick, Improved Feint, Improved Reposition, Improved Steal are now replaced by __Deft Maneuvers__. - You do not provoke an attack of opportunity when performing a trip, disarm, dirty trick, feint, reposition, or steal combat maneuver. In addition, you receive a +2 bonus on checks with these combat maneuvers. Now a prerequisite for the relevant greater combat maneuver feats. - Improved Grapple and Improved Unarmed Strike are combined into __Unarmed Combatant__. - You are considered to be armed even when unarmed - you do not provoke attacks of opportunity when you attack foes while unarmed. Your unarmed strikes now deal 1d6 damage if you are medium sized or 1d4 damage if you are small sized, and you can deal lethal or nonlethal damage, at your choice. You do not provoke an attack of opportunity when performing a grapple combat maneuver. In addition, you receive a +2 bonus on checks made to grapple a foe. You also receive a +2 bonus to your Combat Maneuver Defense whenever an opponent tries to grapple you. - Weapon Focus, Greater Weapon Focus, Weapon Specialization, and Greater Weapon Specialization all apply to entire [groups of fighter weapons](https://www.d20pfsrd.com/classes/core-classes/fighter/#TOC-Weapon-Training-Ex-), as opposed to a single weapon. - Weapon Finesse has been removed as a feat. The “light weapons” category has been renamed to “finesse weapons.” Characters can choose to use either their dexterity bonus or their strength bonus to hit with these weapons, no feat required. “Finesse” is also now a weapon attribute like “brace” or “trip,” allowing a weapon in another category to be finessed (like the rapier). (##) Meta Rules - I expect players in my campaign to be make characters that are generally protagonistic and generally have a desire to adventure in campaign-like settings. No shut-ins or antagonists or exceptionally evil characters are allowed unless these types of characters arise from an explicit event from the campaign itself. This is done so that the party is in agreement on moral issues for the most part and the campaign can go smoothly. This doesn’t mean that every character has to be a lawful good paladin. [For the time being, I am disallowing evil characters unless the campaign is specifically an evil campaign.] - I expect players to be prepared for each session. This will require the players to at least read over the rules for their respective characters and for Pathfinder in general, including class pages and race guides, as well as any special feats, spells, or abilities one might have. You don’t have to have every spell memorized that you are going to use, but at least have it pulled up on a computer or written down in shorthand. I don’t want extra time to be taken looking up spells and feats in combat. I will allow and help with some looking up of rules at the start of a campaign, but later on, you should be able to get the hang of how things work. It’s okay to look up things from time to time, as I as the DM will constantly be doing so from behind the screen. Just try to be respectful of the time we have. - Before the session starts, the DM and players will decide on scheduled breaks throughout the session, as to not greatly interrupt the play with disjointed breaks by individual players. This is to keep the flow of the session going. - The DM and players will also set up an explicit end time for each session. Sessions will not continue past this time IRL, just so that we don’t get too carried away and adventure into content that I don’t have prepared. - I expect players in my campaign to generally follow a somewhat linear adventure path. I will allow for a decent amount of meandering, divergence, and unplanned exploring, as is the nature of the game, but ultimately, the players should expect to follow a pre-planned campaign that I have constructed for them. I will put things in the campaign that will pretty explicitly tell you where to go, and I will give generous hints if I feel the party is lost on where to go. If I ask the party to get back on track of the campaign, I would expect them to do so. - I expect players to pay attention to the events of the session while it is ongoing. When the time for the campaign is decided upon, it should be a time when you don’t have some other engagement to do during the same time, whether it be homework or otherwise. I want the experience to be immersive and interactive, so only minimal activities can be done while playing. If you cannot hold a conversation with someone or play the game in a timely manner while doing the activity, then I would ask that you leave it at home. - If a specific rule is in question during session, and it would take more than a few minutes to look up, then the DM has the right to give a final say for the time being as to be able to continue the campaign without interruption. The rule can be discussed and disputed after the session is over, and a more concrete ruling can be decided between the players and the DM. - Any harmful, bigoted, overly-aggressive, or toxic behavior is not allowed, and players who participate in such activity can be thrown from the campaign at any time at the DM’s discretion.
